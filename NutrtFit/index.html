Get active and be a
healthy weight

Staying active is important for keeping a healthy weight and feeling good. Regular exercise, like walking or cycling, helps burn calories, build muscle, and improve energy. Along with exercise, eating a balanced diet of fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains supports weight control. Aiming for about 150 minutes of activity each week is a great goal to start with. By staying consistent with exercise and healthy eating, you can improve your overall health and prevent many diseases.

Underweight

Diet Plan

High-caloric Intake: Frequently take in calorie-dense food such as nuts, seeds, avocados, and whole grains.
Protein-based Foods: Lean meats, dairy, eggs, and legumes.
Healthy Fats: Include olive oil, nut butters, and fatty fish.
Smoothies and Shakes: Add extra calories by blending fruits, vegetables, and protein powder.
Snacking: Trail mix, cheese, and dried fruits are equally good.

Exercise Plan

Strength Training: Do simple weight training to build up your muscles. Do it 3-4 times in a week.
Cardio: Light cardio such as walking or cycling 20-30 minutes, 2-3 times a week.
Flexibility Exercises: Improve flexibility and prevent injuries by doing yoga or stretching.

Normal Weight

Diet Plan

Balanced Diet: A balanced diet comprising fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats.
Portion Control: Let the portions be in check to avoid excess intake.
Hydration: Ingestion of adequate water, avoiding sweetened drinks.
Frequency of Meals: Take your meals and snacks regularly to maintain your energy level.

Exercise Plan

Cardio: 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio in a week. For example, speed walking or swimming.
Muscle Resistance Training: Exercise 2-3 times a week to maintain muscle mass.
Flexibility and Balance: Do yoga once a week.

Overweight

Diet Plan

Caloric Intake: The overall caloric intake needs to be reduced, with a focus on nutrient-dense foods.
High Fiber Foods: This adds vegetables, fruits, and whole grains to keep the feeling of fullness for a longer period.
Lean Proteins: Lean meats, fish, beans, and legumes.
Healthy Fats: To be used with olive oil, nuts, and seeds in moderation.
Limit Sugars and Refined Carbs: Avoid sugary drinks, sweets, and processed foods.

Exercise Plan

Cardio: 150-300 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week. It also contributes to muscular building, increasing metabolism.
hence, it is recommended 2-3 sessions per week.
Flexibility and Balance: Do yoga to enhance flexibility.

Obesity Class I (BMI 30-34.9)

Diet Plan

Caloric Deficit: Creating a caloric deficit will generally set an environment for weight loss.
Plant-Based Foods: The emphasis should be on fruits, vegetables, whole grains.
Lean Proteins: You can add beans, lentils, lean meats.
Healthy Fats: Avocado-containing foods, nuts, and olive oil-infused foods can be used.
Limit Processed Foods: Limit foods with more calories than nutrients.

Exercise Plan

Cardio: 150-300 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week
Strength Training: 2-3 times a week to build muscle.
Flexibility and Balance: Do flexibility and balance exercises like yoga.

Obesity Class II (BMI 35-39.9)

Diet Plan

Meal Plan: Eat a controlled portion size meal in a structured plan.
High-Fiber Foods: Vegetables, fruits, and whole grains should be emphasized.
Protein foods: This includes lean meats, such as chicken, fish, and legumes.
Healthy Fats: Nuts, seeds, and olive oil are usable.
Limit your intake to sweets, sugary drinks, and refined carbohydrates.

Exercise Plan

Cardio: 150-300 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio every week.
Muscle Workouts: It is recommended to do strength training 2 to 3 times a week for muscle development.
Flexibility and Balance: Add stretching or yoga to help improve flexibility.

Obesity Class III (BMI 40+)

Diet Plan

Medical Oversight: Working with a doctor to come up with the best diet for you.
Low-Calorie, High-Nutrient Foods: Make sure that your diet focuses more on vegetables, fruits, and lean proteins.
Meal Replacement: Using meal replacement shakes or bars-one may need to use these under a doctor's care.
Healthy Fat Sources: Include nuts, seeds, and avocados in your diet.
Frequent Follow-Up: Periodically check on progress and adjust the diet as necessary.

Exercise Plan

Cardio: The low-impact cardio exercise, which could be walking or swimming, should be increased in time and intensity over time.
Strength Training: 2-3 times a week with a focus on bodyweight exercises or light weights.
Flexibility and Balance: Do yoga to help improve flexibility.

Our History

NutrtFit began in 2021 with a simple goal: to help people live healthier lives with clear, expert advice. A small team of nutritionists and fitness enthusiasts saw the confusion around health and diets and wanted to provide trustworthy guidance.

At first, we helped friends and family by creating personalized diet and fitness plans. As word spread, more people came to us for support, which led to the launch of NutrtFit.

Our community grew quickly, and by 2023, we had a team of certified nutritionists, dieticians, and fitness coaches. We focused on offering personalized plans for weight management and healthy living.

Over time, we expanded our services to include a wide range of diet plans, nutrition tips, and fitness programs. We've now helped thousands of people reach their health goals in a sustainable and enjoyable way.

Today, NutrtFit continues to offer expert advice for people at all stages of their health journey. Our mission remains to help everyone live a healthier, happier life with personalized, easy-to-follow guidance.

Diet Plan

For different weight categories, here’s a simple guide: Underweight people should eat more high-calorie foods and lift weights to build muscle. Normal weight individuals should stick to a balanced diet and exercise regularly. Overweight and obese people should cut calories, eat healthier, and exercise more, with the plan becoming more specific as their weight increases.
